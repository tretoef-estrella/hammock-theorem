**Scope.** Proven in full for every k ≥ 1, every tower level q = 3^v, and every coefficient degree e < q. Makes the middle-window syzygy census a fixed, q-independent, finite linear-algebra problem in every even dimension. It is the solving tool for the window census at all k; it does not by itself close the main theorem.

### 0. Setting
`S = F₃[x₀,…,x_{n−1}]`, `n = 2k+2`. `E = (e₁,e₃,…,e_{2k+1})` radical with `E = I(∪_J L_J)` (Radicality, all k); the `(2k+1)!!` sheets `L_J = {x_a+x_b=0 : {a,b}∈J}` over the perfect matchings of `K_n`. `R = S/E`, `q = 3^v`. For an edge `{a,b}`, `V({a,b}) = ∪_{J∋{a,b}} L_J`, `I({a,b}) = I(V({a,b}))`.

**Lemma 0 (edge ideal, all k).** `I({a,b}) = (x_a+x_b) + (ε₁,ε₃,…,ε_{2k−1})`, `ε_j` the j-th elementary symmetric of the `2k` other variables.
*Proof.* Every sheet through the edge lies in `H = {x_a+x_b=0}`; restricted to `H`, its residual `k` pairs form a matching-sheet of `K_{2k}` on the remaining variables. The union of the `(2k−1)!!` such sheets is a cylinder (free in `x_a`) over the `K_{2k}` arrangement, radical ideal `(ε₁,…,ε_{2k−1})` (Radicality in `n−2` variables). Since `S/((x_a+x_b)+I′) ≅ (S′/I′)[x_a]` is reduced, the sum is radical and equals `I(V({a,b}))`. ∎

### 1. The Recognition Theorem
> **Theorem R (all k).** Let `q = 3^v` and `λ = (λ₀,…,λ_{n−1})` homogeneous of degree `e < q`. Then
> `Σ_i λ_i·x_i^q ∈ E ⟺ λ_a − λ_b ∈ I({a,b})` for every edge `{a,b}` of `K_n`.
> The right side has no `q`: **for `e < q`, the window syzygy space is canonically independent of the tower level, in every even dimension.**

*Proof.* `F := Σ λ_i x_i^q ∈ E ⟺ F` vanishes on every sheet (`E` radical). Fix `L_J` with pairs `(a_m,b_m)`, `m=1..k+1`, sheet coordinates `u_m := x_{a_m}|_{L_J}`; then `x_{b_m}^q = −u_m^q` (q odd), so
`F|_{L_J} = Σ_m (λ_{a_m} − λ_{b_m})|_{L_J} · u_m^q`.
Each summand is `u_m^q·g_m` with `deg g_m = e < q`. Two monomials `u_m^q·μ`, `u_l^q·μ′` (`m≠l`, `deg μ,μ′ = e < q`) cannot coincide: agreement needs an exponent `≥ q` in two distinct slots, impossible at total degree `q+e < 2q`. Hence the `k+1` summands have pairwise disjoint monomial supports, and `F|_{L_J}=0 ⟺ (λ_{a_m}−λ_{b_m})|_{L_J}=0` for each `m`. Collecting over the `(2k−1)!!` sheets through a fixed edge gives `λ_a − λ_b ∈ I({a,b})` (Lemma 0). All conditions are linear in degree-`e` forms with no reference to `q`. ∎

**Remark (k-uniform).** The decoupling is pure degree arithmetic (`q+e < 2q`) — never uses the dimension. Lemma 0 is Radicality one dimension down. Neither degrades as `k` grows.

### 2. Corollaries
**Corollary 1 (q-free census).** For each `e ≥ 0`, each `k`, `σ_e(k) := dim Syz_e/(trivial)` is a fixed integer independent of the tower level, and for every `q > e`: `dim (m^{[q]}R)_{q+e} = (n−1)·HF(R)_e − σ_e(k)`.
**Corollary 2 (universal Newton class).** For every `k ≥ 2`, `σ_2(k) = 1`, generated by `λ_i = x_i²`: `λ_a − λ_b = (x_a+x_b)(x_a−x_b) ∈ I({a,b})`. (For `k=1`, `σ_2(1)=3`; the degenerate exception.)

## 4. THE TRIVIAL SUBSPACE AND THE MODULE (COMPLETO — precise definitions)

The census `σ_e := dim Syz_e/(trivial)` uses two objects a referee needs pinned exactly.

### 4.1 The syzygy space
`Syz_e := { λ ∈ (S_e)^n : Σ_i λ_i x_i^q ∈ E }`, the degree-e window syzygies of the tuple `(x_0^q,…,x_{n−1}^q)` modulo `E`. By Theorem R this equals `{ λ ∈ (S_e)^n : λ_a − λ_b ∈ I({a,b}) ∀ edges }` — the **q-free description**.

### 4.2 The trivial subspace, precisely
`Triv_e := { λ ∈ (S_e)^n : λ_i ≡ c (mod E) for a single c ∈ S_e, all i }` — the "constant" (diagonal) tuples, where all coordinates agree modulo `E`. These are trivially syzygies: if `λ_a − λ_b ∈ E ⊆ I({a,b})` for all edges (indeed `λ_a − λ_b ≡ 0 mod E`), the condition holds automatically. `Triv_e ≅ R_e = HF(R)_e` (one free constant `c` mod E). The census counts the **non-trivial** window syzygies:
> **σ_e(k) := dim (Syz_e / Triv_e).**
This matches the anchors: the standard-representation value `σ_3(k) = n−1` is the `(n−1)`-dimensional space of linear tuples `λ_i = α_i·(\text{Newton})` modulo the single diagonal.

### 4.3 q-freeness of the MODULE, not merely the dimension
Theorem R gives more than an equality of dimensions: the **F₃-vector space** `Syz_e/Triv_e` is canonically the solution space of the q-free linear system `{λ_a − λ_b ∈ I({a,b})}`, which does not mention `q`. Hence not only `σ_e(k)` but the entire graded module `M(k) := ⊕_e (Syz_e/Triv_e)` — with its `S`-action by multiplication on the `λ_i` (well-defined since `I({a,b})` are ideals and `Triv` is a submodule) — is **independent of the tower level** for the window `e < q`. This is what licenses "one certified computation seals it for all v" (Slap 1) at the module level: `M(k)` is a fixed finitely generated graded `S`-module, and `σ_e(k)` is its Hilbert function. (Its regularity bound is `THRESHOLD_PARAMETER_COMPLETO` T3.)

## 5. THE BOUNDARY AT e = q (COMPLETO — stated, not skipped)
Theorem R is proven for `e < q`; the decoupling step `q + e < 2q` is exactly the hypothesis. **At `e = q` the disjointness can fail** — two slots can each carry exponent `≥ q` — and the window description no longer holds; this is precisely where the **collar** begins (the Frobenius carry opens a second heavy slot, the "Interruptor" of the slack law). The Recognition Theorem therefore governs the window `0 ≤ e < q` cleanly and hands off to the collar analysis at `e = q`; it makes no claim at or beyond `e = q`, and none is needed from it there. This boundary is not a gap — it is the designed interface between the (q-free) window and the (q-dependent) collar.

**Consolidated statement.** For every `k ≥ 1` and every `q = 3^v`, the graded module `M(k) = ⊕_{e<q}(Syz_e/Triv_e)` is canonically isomorphic to the q-free module defined by the edge conditions `{λ_a−λ_b ∈ I({a,b})}`; its Hilbert function `σ_e(k)` is therefore tower-independent throughout the window, in every even dimension. ∎
