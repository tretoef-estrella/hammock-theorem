## 1. The two numbers are not competitors (the tension, resolved)
The campaign attached two scales to the onset, and their apparent disagreement (`k² = 4, 9` vs `k(k+1) = 6, 12`) was flagged under Ley 48. They measure different things:
- **`k(k+1)` — the regularity/generation ceiling.** The Swap-Ramp scale: the swap product of a sheet has degree `k(k+1)`, and the window syzygy module `M(k)` is generated and related by the swap geometry, so its Castelnuovo–Mumford regularity is bounded by `k(k+1)` (§3). - **`k² — the empirical onset.** The first degree at which the deviation `σ_e − HP(e)` actually reaches 0. Filed: `e₀(2) = 4`, `e₀(3) = 9`, matching `k²`. This is `≤` the regularity ceiling (`k² ≤ k(k+1)` always), and is the sharp value the deviations exhibit.

There is no contradiction: `k² ≤ k(k+1)`, and the census law is guaranteed from the ceiling `k(k+1)` down to the onset `k²` by direct verification on the finite band between them. The Ley-48 caution was right to withhold "onset `= k²` proven"; what is provable is the weaker, sufficient "law holds for `e ≥ k²`."

`σ_e(k) = HF(M(k))(e)` (Recognition + Slap 1: `M(k)` is a fixed finitely generated graded `S`-module whose Hilbert function is the census).
