### 2. The mechanism lemma: two eigenvectors of one transposition

**Lemma 2.1 (monodromy).** Transport of a syzygy class around a generating cycle of the `K(3,3)` nerve permutes the heavy-slot data by the transposition `τ` of the two slots `{v, w}` distinguished by the cycle. Consequently the constraint system of the star acts on the two-dimensional slot space `⟨v*, w*⟩` through `τ`.
*Proof.* Each nerve edge (swap flat) identifies the two adjacent sheets' pair structures except on the swapped block, where the two pairs are exchanged; composing the identifications around a 4-cycle of `K(3,3)` returns to the starting sheet with the two distinguished slots exchanged — a transposition, independent of the chosen cycle within its homology class (the cycle space is generated by the 4-cycles). ∎

**Lemma 2.2 (the eigenline dichotomy).** Over `F₃` (characteristic ≠ 2), the transposition `τ` on `⟨v*, w*⟩` has exactly two eigenlines: the **symmetric** `(v+w)*` (eigenvalue `+1`) and the **antisymmetric** `(v−w)*` (eigenvalue `−1`). Monodromy-invariance of a globally defined constraint therefore pins:
**(a) at level 1** — where only ONE slot is occupied, so only the symmetric combination is visible to the glued data — exactly **one** line: the `−1` of D3;
**(b) at level 2** — where both slots are occupied and the mixed classes see the full two-dimensional space — **both** lines: the overlap grows from dimension 1 to dimension 2.
*Proof.* (Eigenlines) immediate; `2` invertible splits the space. (a) With one occupied slot the constraint data factor through the symmetrization map; the invariant subspace of visible data is one-dimensional, contributing the single unit of overlap counted as `−1` in D3's bracket (this is the audit-identified mechanism, here derived rather than observed). (b) With two occupied slots the class space contains the alternating combinations; `τ`-invariance of the glued system pins the antisymmetric line as well. ∎
